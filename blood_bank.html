Why It Matters
Every 2 seconds, someone needs blood
Blood cannot be manufactured — it can only come from volunteer donors. Your single donation can save up to three lives.
🩸
Whole Blood
The most common type of donation. Your blood is separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets — all used for different patients in need.
🔬
Platelet Donation
Cancer patients and those undergoing organ transplants depend on platelet donations to survive. Platelets must be used within 5 days of donation.
💉
Plasma Donation
Plasma helps patients recover from burns, trauma, and serious illnesses. It can be frozen and stored for up to a year, making it vital for emergencies.
The Process
Donating is simple & fast
The whole process takes under an hour and the actual donation is just 10–12 minutes. Here's what to expect.
1

Register Online

Fill out our quick registration form to check eligibility and schedule your appointment.

2

Health Screening

A brief health check including blood pressure, hemoglobin, and pulse measurement.

3

Donate

The donation itself takes 8–12 minutes. Our trained staff ensures your comfort throughout.

4

Rest & Refresh

Enjoy light snacks and juice before leaving. You're a hero — take your time!

Eligibility Criteria

  • ✓
    Age between 18–65 years
    First-time donors must be under 60 years
  • ✓
    Weight at least 50 kg (110 lbs)
    Body weight affects safe donation volume
  • ✓
    Hemoglobin level ≥ 12.5 g/dL
    Tested during health screening on the day
  • ✓
    No active illness or fever
    Wait 2 weeks after full recovery
  • ✗
    Not donated whole blood in last 56 days
    Platelet donors may donate every 7 days
  • ✗
    Not pregnant or nursing
    Wait 6 weeks after delivery or weaning
  • ✗
    No recent tattoos or piercings (< 1 year)
    Unless done at a licensed facility in certain states
Not sure if you qualify?
Our team can answer any questions about special health conditions, medications, or travel history that may affect eligibility.
